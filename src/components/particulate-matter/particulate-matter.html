What is Particulate Matter? mdi-close

Particulate matter are microscopic solid or liquid matter suspended in the air. They have impacts on climate and precipitation that adversely affect human health, in addition to direct inhalation. Particulate matter smaller than about 10 micrometers, can settle in the bronchi and lungs and cause health problems. Fine particulate matter (PM2.5), tend to penetrate into the gas exchange regions of the lung, and very small particles may pass through the lungs to affect other organs.
